# Embracing the Swiss-Punk Design System

Forget soft rounded corners and pastel SaaS palettes. The new era of design is bold, structural, and unapologetically loud. We call it **Swiss-Punk**.

## What is Swiss-Punk?

It's the collision of two opposing design philosophies:

1. **Swiss Design (International Typographic Style)**: Clean grids, stark sans-serif typography (like *Bricolage Grotesque* and *Bebas Neue*), asymmetry, and objective photography.
2. **Punk Aesthetics**: High-contrast, raw borders, acid colors, monospace technical elements, and a rebellious, anti-corporate feel.

### Core Elements

- **Harsh Borders**: 2px solid ink black borders around everything. It grounds elements and creates a tactile, blueprint-like feel.
- **Acid Colors**: Neon green (`#b8ff00`) acts as a highlighter over stark off-white paper backgrounds (`#f5f2e8`).
- **Typography as Architecture**: Text isn't just content; it's structure. Giant, uppercase headers lead the eye, while microscopic tracking on monospace labels provides technical texture.
